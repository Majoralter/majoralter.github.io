# The thing about resolutions
It's that time again when people outline all the things that they'll do to make the new year a memorable and/or productive one. But what usually happens is that they forget all about those resolutions halfway through January and haphazardly let the year slowly pass by. Now the problem isn't setting goals or having resolutions, it's how those goals or resolutions are set.
<br />
# Yet another year
I've been setting goals at the start of each year since 2022, but I usually let them die in my notes app never to be seen again. I want this year to be different, seriously I really do. So the system I'm going to follow is to set goals for each quarter that will carry-over into the next quarter. I'm going to set goals for each month at the start of that month (that means I'm going to regularly update this post).
<br />
## January
My goals for this month:
* I've been working as a solo frontend dev on a client project since September and I want to finally be free from this project. Preferrably in the first couple of weeks of this month.
* I want to start my project/startup that I've been planning with a friend since August-ish of last year.
* I'm currently reading 3 books (weird, I know) and I want to finish them. The books are The lost world, Howl's moving castle and Mistborn: The final empire.
* I really want to take more pictures this year (my potato phone makes me forget that the camera app exists) so I might as well start now. 

## January review
I'm writing this in March because, somehow, we are already in March. The first quarter of the year is almost over. I didn't achieve my goals for January the client project needed restructuring on the backend, and after a review, they requested a UI revamp (of sorts). So, the project has been extended indefinitely again.  

I can't speak much about the project because we haven't launched yet, etc.  

The startup idea isn't dead, but I just don't have the time to fully (or even partially) commit to something that big yet.  

I managed to finish *The Lost World*, and I actually enjoyed it. The theories it touched on were really intriguing, and it was overall a good book nothing mind-blowing, though. I didn't finish the first *Mistborn* book or *Howl's Moving Castle* because my brain just couldn't handle reading for a while.

Finally, I took just one picture in January. So yeah, slow month.
